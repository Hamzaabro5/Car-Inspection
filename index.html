A Vehicle Identification Number (VIN) is a unique 17-character alphanumeric code assigned to every motor vehicle. You can find your VIN on the driver's side dashboard (visible through the windshield), the driver's door jamb sticker, your vehicle registration, or your insurance card.
Our reports are informational tools sourced from official government and insurance databases. While they are not legal documents, they are widely accepted by dealers, lenders, and insurance companies as credible reference documents. For legal title disputes, consult your state's DMV.
We sync with our 60+ data partners on a rolling basis — most records are updated within 30–90 days of being reported to state agencies. Accident and insurance data is typically the fastest-updating, while some DMV title records may have a longer processing window depending on the state.
The National Motor Vehicle Title Information System (NMVTIS) is a federal database maintained by the U.S. Department of Justice. Being NMVTIS certified means Drive Audit Pro is an authorized provider that accesses this database directly, giving you access to official title and branding records from all 50 states.
A "no records found" result is generally a positive sign — it means no accidents, title issues, or odometer discrepancies have been reported to our data sources. However, it does not guarantee a perfect vehicle. Some incidents may not have been reported to insurance or authorities. We always recommend a physical inspection for high-value purchases.
